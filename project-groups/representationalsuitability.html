An Investigation into the Representational Suitability of Tree Visualizations

Abstract

With a constant need for dashboards and visual analytic systems to efficiently explore and monitor business-related data, business Intelligence (BI) remains one of the most prevalent application domains for data visualization. In this series of projects, I explored effectiveness of visual representations to track changes in dashboards and also developed end-to-end visual analytic systems that allow exploring alliances and enterprise ecosystems to make critical business decisions (e.g. helping venture capitals decide which startups to invest in). These systems have hundreds of monthly users including both decision makers at industrial firms and researchers at business schools (including Stanford Graduate School of Business, Darden School of Business, Fisher College of Business, and Babson College, among others).
